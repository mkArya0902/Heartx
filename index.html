Cardiac Anatomy

🫀
Four Chambers
Heart has 4 chambers: 2 atria (upper, receiving) and 2 ventricles (lower, pumping). Separated by interventricular septum. Left side handles oxygenated blood, right side handles deoxygenated blood.
Anatomy
🔄
Heart Valves
Tricuspid (3 leaflets, RA→RV) · Mitral (2 leaflets, LA→LV) · Pulmonary (3 cusps, RV→PA) · Aortic (3 cusps, LV→Aorta). All prevent backflow.
Valvular
🩸
Great Vessels
Aorta (out of LV) · Pulmonary Trunk (out of RV) · Superior & Inferior Vena Cava (into RA) · Pulmonary Veins — 4 veins (into LA). These are the major pipes connecting heart to circulation.
Vascular
🛡️
Pericardium
Double-layered sac surrounding the heart. Fibrous pericardium (outer, tough) and serous pericardium (inner). Pericardial fluid (20–50 mL) reduces friction during heartbeats.
Protective
🔬
Heart Wall Layers
Epicardium — outer visceral layer · Myocardium — thick middle muscular layer (does the pumping) · Endocardium — smooth inner lining. Myocardium is thickest in the left ventricle.
Histology
💛
Coronary Circulation
Heart muscle gets its own blood supply from coronary arteries that originate from the aortic root. Venous drainage via coronary sinus into right atrium. Maximum blood flow occurs in diastole (relaxation phase).
Circulation
Function

Cardiac Physiology

01

Cardiac Cycle

Systole — ventricles contract, blood pumped out (~0.3 sec). Diastole — ventricles relax, fill with blood (~0.5 sec). Normal resting HR = 60–100 bpm. Each cycle lasts ~0.8 sec at 75 bpm.

~0.8 sec / cycle
02

Conduction System

SA Node (pacemaker, 60–100/min) → AV Node (delays impulse) → Bundle of His → Left & Right Bundle Branches → Purkinje fibers (rapid spread to ventricles). SA node is in right atrium near SVC opening.

Electrical
03

Heart Sounds

S1 ("Lub") — closure of Mitral + Tricuspid valves at start of systole. S2 ("Dub") — closure of Aortic + Pulmonary valves at end of systole. S3 = rapid filling (may be normal in young), S4 = atrial kick (pathological).

Auscultation
04

ECG Basics

P wave = atrial depolarization · QRS = ventricular depolarization · T wave = ventricular repolarization. PR interval 0.12–0.20s, QRS <0.12s. ST elevation → MI!

ECG
05

Cardiac Output

CO = Heart Rate × Stroke Volume. Normal CO ≈ 5 L/min at rest. Stroke Volume ~70 mL. Can increase to 25 L/min during intense exercise. Ejection Fraction (EF) = SV/EDV × 100. Normal EF >55%.

Hemodynamics
06

Frank-Starling Law

The more the heart is filled during diastole (preload), the stronger it contracts during systole. Up to a physiological limit. This is the heart's intrinsic auto-regulation mechanism — no nervous system needed.

Mechanism
Coronary Arteries

Blood Supply

Right Coronary Artery (RCA)

Originates from right aortic sinus. Supplies SA node (60% cases), AV node, right atrium, right ventricle, inferior LV. Branches: Posterior Descending Artery (PDA) in right-dominant hearts.

Right Dominant in 70% people

Left Coronary Artery (LCA)

Short main stem (0.5–1 cm) arising from left aortic sinus. Quickly divides into LAD (Left Anterior Descending) and Circumflex artery. Supplies most of the left ventricle = most of the pumping muscle.

Most critical supply

LAD — Widow Maker

Left Anterior Descending Artery. Supplies anterior LV wall, anterior septum, apex of heart, and bundle branches. Called "Widow Maker" — blockage causes massive anterior MI with high mortality.

High risk vessel

Circumflex Artery (LCx)

Branch of LCA. Runs in left AV groove. Supplies posterior and lateral LV wall, left atrium. In left-dominant hearts (10–15%), it gives posterior descending artery. Supplies SA node in 40% cases.

Lateral wall

⚠ Clinical Pearl — Myocardial Infarction

When a coronary artery is blocked by a thrombus (blood clot) on a ruptured atherosclerotic plaque, the heart muscle it supplies stops getting oxygen. Within 20–40 minutes, irreversible myocardial necrosis (cell death) begins. This is a STEMI (ST-Elevation MI) or NSTEMI based on ECG findings. Presentation: crushing chest pain radiating to left arm/jaw, sweating, breathlessness. Emergency treatment = PCI (angioplasty) or thrombolysis within the "golden hour."

Pathology

Cardiac Diseases

Critical

Myocardial Infarction

Blockage of coronary artery → ischemia → necrosis of heart muscle. Classic symptoms: chest pain, radiation to jaw/arm, sweating, breathlessness. Troponin rises in 3–4 hours.

⚡ ECG: ST elevation, new LBBB
Serious

Heart Failure

Heart unable to pump enough blood to meet body's needs. Can be systolic (low EF <40%) or diastolic (preserved EF). Features: breathlessness, leg swelling, fatigue, orthopnoea (can't lie flat).

⚡ BNP elevated · CXR: pulmonary oedema
Common

Hypertension

BP persistently >140/90 mmHg. "Silent killer" — usually no symptoms until organ damage. Long-term: LV hypertrophy, stroke, kidney failure, aortic dissection. Lifestyle + antihypertensives.

⚡ Target organ damage: brain, heart, kidney, eye
Variable

Arrhythmia

Abnormal heart rhythm. Types: AF (most common, irregularly irregular), VF (life-threatening), heart block, SVT, WPW. Symptoms: palpitations, dizziness, syncope, sudden death in severe cases.

⚡ AF: risk of stroke → anticoagulate!
Chronic

Valvular Disease

Stenosis (narrowing) or Regurgitation (leaking) of heart valves. Aortic stenosis most common in elderly. Mitral regurgitation causes volume overload. Rheumatic fever → mitral stenosis in young.

⚡ Murmur on auscultation → Echo for diagnosis
Genetic

Cardiomyopathy

Disease of heart muscle. DCM (dilated) — flabby, enlarged heart, reduced EF. HCM (hypertrophic) — thickened septum, cause of sudden death in young athletes. RCM — stiff heart, diastolic failure.

⚡ HCM → sudden cardiac death in athletes

Heart Facts

💓
100,000

Your heart beats about 100,000 times every single day — that is about 3 billion beats in a lifetime without ever stopping to rest!

🩸
7,500 L

The heart pumps approximately 7,500 litres of blood every day — enough to fill a large swimming pool in one year.

⚡
60 W

The electrical energy your heart generates in a day could power a small LED light bulb for 24 hours. Your heart is truly a biological battery.

🔴
96,000 km

Your blood vessels — arteries, veins, and capillaries — if laid end to end would stretch around the Earth more than twice!

🥊
300 g

An average adult heart weighs only about 250–350 grams — roughly the size of your own fist. Yet it is the most powerful muscle in your body.

👶
22 Days

A baby's heart begins beating just 22 days after conception — even before the mother knows she is pregnant. It starts as a simple tube that folds and loops.
